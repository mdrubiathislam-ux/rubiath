About Me

Researcher at the interface of microbes, genes, and host biology

From molecular microbiology to host-focused biomedical research

My academic journey connects microbiology, molecular biology, wastewater microbiome research, antimicrobial resistance surveillance, immunology, stem cell biology, and computational analysis. I completed both my BSc and MS in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology from Shahjalal University of Science and Technology, Bangladesh, and I am now beginning my PhD training in Quantitative Biology at the University of Texas at Arlington.

My research background includes hospital wastewater metagenomics, ARG surveillance, integron and transposon analysis, bacterial isolation, antimicrobial susceptibility testing, PCR, qPCR, RT-PCR, Sanger sequencing, and bioinformatics pipeline development. I am especially interested in how microbial systems influence disease, immunity, and therapeutic opportunities.
